# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why can't trans people just continue wearing clothes from their birth sex aisle?
Because that's not how gendered fashion works unfortunately!
- Post-transitional bodies will have different shapes than they had pre-transition (ex. a transmasc who's had top surgery won't fit women's tops anymore cause women's tops have extra space for boobs built into them)
- Men's and women's fashion aim to create different shape language/proportion, which means the way they're cut & worn, even beyond actual sizing, differs (ex. women's fashion uses the natural waist while men's uses the high hip as its waist - in my case that is a 10cm difference in circumference!)
    - For trans people specifically, wearing gendered fashion appropriately is also extremely important to mitigate misgendering risk, so there's not a lot of wiggle room to defy those conventions and get away with it. (ex. I need to wear my pants on my high hip because if I wear them any higher, I am more likely to get misgendered. If a cis guy does it he might look a bit funny but he likely wouldn't be straight up mistaken for a woman because of it.)

## Why no pre-med respondents?
Unmodified trans bodies would have the same measurements as the cis bodies we already have general population anthropometry data on. What we don't have (appropriately labelled) anthropometry data on is post-transitional trans bodies.

## Why specifically 3 years on HRT?
It takes around 3-5 years for all the meat changes (ex. muscle and fat redistribution, transfemme breastgrowth) to finalise. We want respondents with stable/final measurements.
