# Data Management Plan
[last updated March 2026]

Ren Zoller is in charge of all aspects of this project, including data management.

## What kind of data are you generating
- A set of questionnaire responses
- A set of standard measurements to use to pattern draft garments for trans people
- Software to clean and process the former dataset into the latter
- Pattern drafting methods for a transmasc suit and some block patterns
- Software to test existing pattern drafting methods with different measurements
- Work wear guidance
- Any supporting documentation for the project
- Any pictures of garments made for this project

## How much data will you generate
The survey responses will be determined by the number of respondents. We expect a few hundred to a few thousand if the survey goes especially viral (it won't lmao). For reference, [much more established gender diverse surveys](https://www.gendercensus.com/) get up to 50k responses which make for a single ~20MB csv file, so this one will get nowhere near that big.

Standard measurements usually make a page-sized table per each set. There will be measurements for regular size, plus size, extra tall, and petit(e) for either transition direction.

Pattern drafting methods and the guidance should be a PDF document with text and illustrations each.

The software will be some code files organised in an appropriate folder structure.

Supporting documentation will be a few text files in relevant formats.

Questionnaire images, flyers, photos taken, and any illustrations for the drafting methods and guidance will be jpeg or png files.

## What standards and methodologies will you use?
For methodology, please see our [methodology](methodology.md) document.

## Is the data sensitive? How will this be managed?
Any sensitive data, like age or medical information, will be collected as generically as possible.

All responses will be fully anonymous.

No participant will be individually identifyable based on their published responses. 

Participants will be asked to confirm their informed consent to participate before undertaking the survey.

## Where will the data be stored?
The active survey is hosted on google forms.

After the survey concludes, the responses will be backed up to [a repository on github](https://github.com/dkettchen/trans_standard_sizing), where all other project data (ie code etc) will also be stored. 

The final data will be publicly available on [github](https://github.com/dkettchen/trans_standard_sizing) indefinitely. The published survey data and results will likely also be available on relevant other repository sites.

All results (standard measurements, drafting methods, guidance) will also be shared across social media and [Ren's website](https://www.dkettchen.com).

The work wear guidance will also be sent to various lgbt organisations that have resource lists to add it to.

## Will the data be valuable for future research?
Any datasets on small minority groups like the trans community are rare and therefore invaluable.

The survey's aim is to generate a dataset that is not only useful to this project's practical goal of assessing trans proportions and producing trans standard sizing, but also hopefully to future research about trans people's relationship to standard sizing in their birthsex aisle pre-transition and in their target gender aisle post-transition. 

As it is anthropometry data, it may also be useful to other fields of research, like medical science.

However due to the limited aims of this project and the rarity of our target demographic (surveys on cis people would be able to easily find enough respondents in any country on its own, but trans people make up less than 0.5% of the general population even in the most lgbt+-tolerant countries), we did not go out of our way to try and make the dataset representative of the entire trans community beyond what is needed for the project, so it should not be assumed to be representative of any aspect outside of those relevant to the scope of the project as defined in our [methodology doc](methodology.md).
